## Email draft (EN)

Subject: **Free LA tenant-rights estimator — request for a legal-accuracy read-through (not representation)**

Hi [name / clinic],

I'm an independent developer in Los Angeles. I built **RentRights**
(https://rentrights.writingdeveloper.blog), a free, open-source, English/Spanish web tool
that estimates — from a street address and public parcel records — whether a
renter's home is likely covered by the City of LA RSO, AB 1482, LA County's
RSTPO, or just-cause-only rules, and what the current annual increase cap is.

It is deliberately positioned as **legal information, not legal advice**
(JustFix-style): every result is labeled an estimate, dated, and points the
renter to LAHD/DCBA and free legal aid — including [organization].

Before promoting it publicly, I'd be grateful for a **legal-accuracy
read-through** by someone with LA landlord-tenant expertise. I've prepared a
short review packet: every figure with its citation, the three riskiest copy
decisions, and four open questions (about 20–30 minutes to skim). I'm not
seeking representation or a formal opinion — corrections and red flags at
whatever depth you can offer, and I'll credit the review or keep it anonymous,
as you prefer.

The tool is free, has no ads or accounts, stores nothing, and the code is
public at github.com/writingdeveloper/rentrights.

Would someone at [organization] be willing to take a look? I can share the
packet as a PDF or a Google Doc, whichever is easier.

Thank you for the work you do for LA tenants,

Si Hyeong Lee
[add your contact email] · https://rentrights.writingdeveloper.blog
