## Community presence — start authentic, over WEEKS not in the sprint (NOT spam)

- **Best subs:** r/AskLosAngeles, r/Tenant. **r/LosAngeles = comments only.** **r/legaladvice = do NOT link** (referrals banned — use it to learn the exact phrasings renters use).
- **The rule that keeps you safe (90/10):** ≥90% of your account activity is genuine, non-promotional help; ≤10% touches the tool. Build a few weeks of real answer history first; never post the same comment twice; disclose "I built this free tool"; link **last**, and only when the tool answers exactly what the asker wants. Explicit "I made this" posts on r/AskLosAngeles go in the **monthly Open Discussion** pinned thread.
- **Help-first snippet template (customize every time — never paste verbatim):**
  > Short version: your building is most likely covered by **[RSO / AB 1482 / LA County]**, which caps annual increases at about **[cap]** right now and requires just cause to evict. A [X]% jump like that [is/looks] over the cap — your landlord can only go up to about **$[max]**. Confirm for free with **[LAHD / DCBA]** before you act.
  > Full disclosure, I built a free, no-signup, open-source tool that estimates this from your exact address (it's an estimate, not legal advice): https://rentrights.writingdeveloper.blog
- **Spanish:** answer Spanish-language "¿es legal este aumento?" posts in Spanish — that's an under-served edge.
- **One-time evergreen:** write a single thorough **Quora** answer to "how much can a landlord raise rent in LA/CA?" citing the tool as a free address checker.
